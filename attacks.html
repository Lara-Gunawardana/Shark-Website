Reasons behind Shark attacks

Sharks, which use vision as one of many senses, may mistake the silhouette of a human or surfboard for natural prey like seals, sea turtles, or fish.

Shark attack statistics show that encounters are extremely rare despite intense media coverage. sharks kill fewer than 10 people worldwide per year.The likelihood of a fatal shark attack is roughly 1 in 4.3 million. Statistically, you are far more likely to die from drowning (1 in 1,134) or even a lightning strike. In fact you are even more likely to die by champagne cork. One study estimated that around 24 humans die each year in France from incidents involving champagne corks.

attacks on sharks

shark finning

Humans kill up to 100 million sharks every year. This equals roughly to 10,000–20,000 sharks killed every hour. The vast majority of these deaths are a resu;t of shark finning. Shark finning is the brutal practice of removing a shark's fins and discarding the rest of the body—often while the shark is still alive—at sea. The mutilated shark is then unable to swim and dies of suffocation, blood loss, or predation. Despite a 10-fold increase in regulations over the last decade, total shark deaths have increased.

Protecting sharks is essential for maintaining healthy oceans, as they play a key role in balancing marine ecosystems and supporting ocean life.
